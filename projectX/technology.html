I. The printing press

The machine devised by the German Gutenberg in the mid-15th century enabled the mass production of books. As a result, it led to the spread of ideas (especially religious ones), the spread of knowledge, literacy and the creation of libraries in Europe.

II. The steam engine

It was invented by the Scottish engineer James Watt in 1775 - revolutionised transport and machinery in the 19th century and drove the First Industrial Revolution, rapidly moving from an economy based on agriculture and trade to an industrialised one with much greater production capacity.

III. The Light Bulb

Thomas Edison considered to be inventor of light bulb in 1880. It is one of the greatest invention since the discovery of fire: light entered homes and workplaces, becoming a necessity and an engine for economic growth

IV. The telephone

Alexander Graham Bell patented this device in 1876, which revolutionised communication by allowing instant speech even over long distances. Without it, the world would not exist as we know it today: it laid the foundations for mobile telephony.

V. The aeroplane

In 1903, the Wright brothers created the first human-piloted motorised aeroplane, the Wright Flyer. This technological ingenuity boosted trade, culture, tourism and, today, the air transport industry is key to global economic prosperity.

VI. The computer

The first personal computer with a microprocessor was the Micral (1973). Computers have redefined people’s lives and the way they work, simplifying tasks, storing information and processing data quickly and efficiently.

VII. The internet

The connection of four university computers to ARPAnet in 1969 was the seed for the birth of the Internet. This breakthrough was key to Tim Berners-Lee’s introduction of the World Wide Web in 1991, transforming society. It continues to evolve today, bringing new forms of interaction and economic, social and cultural growth.

VIII. The mobile phone

In 1983, the first mobile phone small enough to be portable was launched: Motorola DynaTac 8000X, designed by engineer Martin Cooper. Today it is one of the essential technological inventions in personal and professional life.

IX. Artificial intelligence

The precursor of modern computing, Alan Turing, is also the father of artificial intelligence. However, the term was not coined until 1956, when the first artificial intelligence programme, Logic Theorist. Today, this technological invention has crept into our lives in the form of chatbots, voice assistants, autonomous vehicles, real-time translators, ChatGPT, IoT.
